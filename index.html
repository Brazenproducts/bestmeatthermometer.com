❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is safe for chicken?

The USDA recommends cooking chicken to an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). Check the thickest part of the thigh, away from bone. With an instant-read thermometer this takes 3 seconds — no guessing required.

What's the difference between instant-read and leave-in thermometers?

Instant-read thermometers (Alpha Grillers, Thermapen, etc.) give a quick spot-check temperature in seconds — perfect for steaks, burgers, and chicken breasts. Leave-in thermometers (ThermoPro TP-16) stay inserted throughout the cook, alerting you when the meat reaches your target — perfect for roasts, briskets, and turkeys. Ideally, own one of each.

How accurate does my meat thermometer need to be?

For home cooking, ±1–2°F is perfectly fine. The FDA's safe-temperature guidelines include a safety margin far larger than that. Only competition cooks and food professionals need the ±0.5°F accuracy of the Thermapen ONE — though it's nice to have.

Can I leave a meat thermometer in the oven while cooking?

Only if it's specifically designed for it. Instant-read thermometers like the Alpha Grillers or Thermapen are not oven-safe and should be removed after each check. The ThermoPro TP-16 is designed with a heat-resistant cord and probe that stays in through the entire cook.

How do I calibrate a meat thermometer?

Most digital thermometers come pre-calibrated. To verify, use the ice water method: fill a glass with ice water, insert the probe, and check the reading — it should be 32°F (0°C). If it's off, many thermometers have a recalibration button. The ThermoPro TP19H and Thermapen ONE are both factory-calibrated and rarely need adjustment.
